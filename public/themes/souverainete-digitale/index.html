Ask a question
What does digital sovereignty actually mean?
It's an organization's ability to fully control its data, infrastructure and digital workloads, shielded from non-European jurisdictions (CLOUD Act, FISA…). In practice: EU hosting, encryption, in-house key management and strong contractual clauses.
How long does a typical sovereign migration take?
Depending on complexity: between 3 and 12 months. Engagements start with a 4-to-6-week audit, followed by phased rollouts in functional batches to minimize operational risk.
Can we keep our existing Microsoft / Google tools?
Yes for non-critical use cases. For sensitive data, we offer equivalent European alternatives or hybrid architectures with client-side encryption that preserves confidentiality.
Which industries do you work with most?
Public sector, healthcare, banking and finance, defense, advanced industry and energy. Our method adapts to each sector's regulatory constraints (HDS, ACPR, ANSSI, etc.).
Is the cost significantly higher than a U.S. public cloud?
On optimized workloads, the gap is now marginal (5-15%). On stable workloads, some clients see savings thanks to no egress fees and multi-year commitments.
And if we want to leave one day — is reversibility real?
Absolutely. Reversibility is contractually guaranteed: open standards, portable formats, exit support included, no hidden fees and no penalty delays.
